Question
Why do we need to do Business Process Re-engineering as a part of implementing an EHR? Note: Your answer should be at minimum 4 to 5 sentences and must be in your own words.
Answer must be at minimum 4 to 5 sentences and in the student's own words.

Grading Rubric
Up to 6 Points for Points: 6. Purpose: Assess whether the student names specific, relevant process issues that would motivate BPR in an EHR implementation and succinctly characterizes why each is problematic. Exact point mapping: - Excellent (6 points): Names 3 or more distinct, directly relevant issues and gives a brief, accurate characterization for each (example issues: workflow bottlenecks, redundant data entry, poor data reuse, interoperability gaps, misalignment with mission, regulatory compliance risk). - Good (4 points): Names 2 relevant issues with reasonable specificity and brief characterization. - Adequate (2 points): Names 1 relevant issue clearly, or names multiple issues but descriptions are vague or partially incorrect. - Poor (0 points): Issues are missing, incorrect, or irrelevant. Notes: Do NOT deduct here for mitigation/explanation text that properly belongs to the Explanation criterion; focus on presence and correctness of named issues and their brief characterization.
Up to 8 Points for Points: 8. Purpose: Evaluate the quality of causal reasoning linking BPR actions to mitigation of the identified issues and the expected benefits in the EHR context. Exact point mapping: - Excellent (8 points): Provides clear, accurate causal links that show HOW BPR will address the named issues (e.g., redesigning workflows eliminates redundant steps and reduces bottlenecks; standardizing data capture improves interoperability and data reuse) and explicitly states at least two distinct benefit areas (examples: improved decision-making, interoperability, reduced costs, alignment with strategy, simplified procurement). - Good (6 points): Provides correct causal links for most named issues and mentions one or two benefit areas, but with less depth or fewer concrete examples. - Adequate (4 points): Offers general or partial explanations that indicate basic understanding (e.g., 'BPR makes processes more efficient') but lacks specific linkage to the named issues or concrete benefits. - Poor (0 points): Explanations are incorrect, irrelevant, or missing. Notes: Prioritize correct conceptual linking; concise but accurate causal reasoning should receive high credit. If mitigation is present only as a very short clause (<=10 words) attached to a named issue, limit Explanation credit for that linkage to up to 50% of the Explanation points for that linkage unless additional reasoning is present.
Up to 2 Points for Points: 2. Purpose: Determine whether the student expressed ideas in their own words as required. This criterion is independent of conceptual quality. Use the objective thresholds below (use similarity reports when available). Exact point mapping: - Full (2 points): Similarity < 10% AND no contiguous verbatim segments of 4 or more words copied from provided or external sources (excluding standard technical terms). - Partial (1 point): Similarity 10–30% OR limited contiguous verbatim phrase(s) of 4–7 words present, but most of the response is in the student's own words. - None (0 points): Similarity > 30% OR contiguous verbatim segments of 8 or more words OR near-verbatim complete sentences/paragraphs. Procedure: Attach similarity-report excerpt when using a tool. If a tool is not available, perform a manual check for contiguous verbatim passages and escalate borderline cases to a second grader or administrator. For 0-point findings, document evidence and notify the course administrator within 48 hours; mark the submission 'under originality review' (conceptual scores may be recorded but final academic-integrity penalties are determined by the institutional office). Tie-breaker: prioritize contiguous-word check for short verbatim segments when percent and contiguous-word tests conflict; if unresolved, escalate and document the rationale.
Maximum of 16 Points

Originality (originality_paraphrase) = independent assessment of whether the response is in the student's own words, using the objective thresholds in the overall instructor guidelines.
